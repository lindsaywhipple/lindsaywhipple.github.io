About Me

Hello! I’m Lindsay Whipple — a curious and driven data analyst with a deep interest in how numbers can tell meaningful stories. I bring a unique blend of analytical rigor and human understanding, shaped by my academic background and professional experience.

I graduated from St. Lawrence University with a double major in Statistics and Psychology, where I developed a strong foundation in both quantitative analysis and behavioral insight. During my time there, I captained the varsity squash team and studied abroad in Stockholm, Sweden — an experience that broadened my perspective and deepened my appreciation for cultural and academic exploration.

Professionally, I’ve spent the past several years at Bitsight Technologies, where I’ve grown from an entry-level analyst into a cross-functional contributor specializing in technical research, cybersecurity risk, and data integrity. My work has spanned everything from analyzing large-scale data breaches to maintaining the accuracy of digital infrastructure mappings — always with an eye for detail and a commitment to quality.

To deepen my expertise, I’m currently pursuing a Master’s in Applied Data Analytics part-time at Boston University. I’m especially interested in using data to drive smarter decisions, uncover blind spots, and make complex topics more accessible through clear communication and thoughtful design.

Outside the world of data, you can usually find me traveling, exploring the outdoors, or hanging with my two dogs, Otis and Asher. Some recent favorite destinations include Scottsdale, Iceland, and Lisbon. Scroll through a few snapshots below!
